power system stability

Power system stability is the property of a power system that enables it to remain in a state of operating equilibrium under normal operating conditions and to regain an acceptable state of equilibrium after being subjected to a disturbance.

  1. transient stability
  2. rotor angle stability.

faults in a power system

  • Generator failure resulting from insulation break down between turns in the same slot or between windings and te steel structure of the machines
  • For the case of overhead lines since they have bear conductor, wind, birds, trees and damage of the support structures are a cause of faults
  • Contamination of insulators and lightning overvoltage lead to short circuits
  • Deterioration of insulation in underground cables also results into short circuits. This is mainly attributed to ageing and overloading of these cables
  • They can also arise from mal operation by the network operators
